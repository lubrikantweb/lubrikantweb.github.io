Episode 5 - 'Wild Dreams' by Kolya

Casper Clark / Volte-Face · 💦LYLR 5💦 Wild Dreams by Kolya

WORDS FROM KOLYA
“Dreams, dreams wild dreams!” This audiobook (!) starts off woozily and kinda boozily in some late-80s American locker room - hellooo high-school highballs! Soon the thick funk retreats and the heavy smog of minor chords loom - oh hello, interiority? Thank god that’ll never return (!?) Anyways, pretty soon we’re up in the sky riding some magic tea towel, with all kinds of ups and downs - whooosh! If cats are dreaming about their owners, and I certainly try to access my feline potential, I guess my dreaming self is owned by some sort of rollercoaster factory!! if you catch my gist?! - “Midnight fantasy!”

WORDS FROM CASPER CLARK
Well now, this really is a special one! Not only have I never heard a single track on this mixtape before (always a good sign, and just the way we like it Chez Lubrikant), but it’s also put together by a dear friend whose singular ear has been apparent since our first encounters at the Lock Tavern some 20 odd years ago when he was but an eager chicken. It’s a diverse journey through obscurant corners of the electronicasphere, with a consistently exotic, woozy… dare I say it, dreamy vibe. You won’t hear anything else quite like it any time soon.

LYLR 002

Casper Clark / Volte-Face · 💦LYLR 4💦 Walking Straight Ahead In A Backwards Direction by Lucy Swan

WORDS FROM LUCY SWAN
While putting this together I was thinking on going absolutely nowhere as a journey in and of itself; on repetition as a form of change; on stasis as progress; on devotion as practice; on the quiet resilience of love etc.

WORDS FROM CASPER CLARK It became apparent very early on in curating this series that I should limit it (almost) exclusively to people that I have an existing friendship with. For the fourth instalment, I've taken the premise a little further by inviting and encouraging a friend who had never put together and shared a 'mix' before. Her contribution tested my own limits by exploring non-electronic alternative, experimental and minimal ground that I have never particularly explored in my own mixes.

LUCY SWAN BIO
Lucy is an interdisciplinary writer, not to be confused with the author behind 'Shallow Lives', whose work sits at the intersection of critical theory, creative writing and visual art. Her work has been published and exhibited internationally. Lucy has a secondhand bookselling project; SIREN BOOKS and is working on her first book of essays; Getting Cosy.
